The Legend of a True King

Shiv Jayanti marks the birth of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the founder of the Maratha Empire. It's a day to remember his courage, his revolutionary administrative and military strategies, and his unwavering commitment to 'Swaraj' - self-rule. This website is a humble tribute to his indomitable spirit.

The Life of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

A journey through the key milestones of a legendary life.

1630 - The Birth of a Legend

Born in the hill-fort of Shivneri, Shivaji was instilled with the ideals of dharma and self-respect by his mother, Jijabai, and his mentor, Dadoji Konddeo.

1645 - The Oath of Swaraj

At the tender age of 15, at Raireshwar temple, Shivaji and his loyal friends took a solemn oath to establish 'Hindavi Swaraj' - a self-governing kingdom of the people.

1659 - The Victory at Pratapgad

In a legendary encounter, Shivaji Maharaj strategically defeated the formidable Adilshahi general, Afzal Khan, showcasing his tactical genius and bravery.

1674 - The Coronation

Shivaji Maharaj was formally crowned 'Chhatrapati' (sovereign) at Raigad fort, establishing the Maratha Empire as a legitimate and powerful entity.

1680 - The Legacy Begins

Though his reign was short, his death marked the beginning of an immortal legacy that inspired generations to fight for justice and freedom.

The Mighty Maratha Empire

From a small jagir to a pan-Indian empire.

Rise of an Empire

Founded by Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj, the Maratha Empire was known for its mobility, guerrilla warfare tactics, and a strong network of sea and land forts. It challenged the might of the Mughal Empire and, at its peak, controlled a vast territory across the Indian subcontinent.

The empire was a testament to Shivaji's vision of a state run with principles of justice, religious tolerance, and efficient administration, a model far ahead of its time.

Pillars of the Empire

Brave hearts who carried the saffron flag high.

Chhatrapati Sambhaji Maharaj

The valiant son of Shivaji Maharaj, a scholar and warrior who fiercely defended the empire against overwhelming odds.

Maharani Tarabai

The regent queen who led the Maratha resistance against the Mughals with incredible courage and strategic acumen.

Peshwa Bajirao I

An undefeated general who vastly expanded the Maratha Empire through his brilliant military campaigns.

Tanaji Malusare

A close companion of Shivaji, whose legendary bravery in the Battle of Sinhagad is immortalized in Maratha folklore.

The Sentinel Forts

The unbreachable hill forts that were the backbone of Swaraj.

Raigad

The capital of the Maratha Empire, where Shivaji Maharaj was crowned.

Sinhagad

The 'Lion's Fort', won by Tanaji Malusare in a historic battle.

Pratapgad

Witness to the historic victory against Afzal Khan.

An Everlasting Legacy

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj was more than a king; he was a nation-builder. His principles of administration, naval power, respect for all religions, and empowerment of the common man continue to inspire India. His life is a powerful reminder that with courage and a just cause, one can challenge the greatest of empires.
